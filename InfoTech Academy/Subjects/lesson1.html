    IT101 - Introduction to Computer Networks


    UNIT I: Networking Basics and Terminology

    This topic introduces the fundamental concepts of computer networking. It covers key terms such as IP address, MAC address, routers, switches, and protocols like TCP/IP. You'll learn how data travels across networks, the difference between LAN and WAN, and the role of network devices in enabling communication between computers.


    UNIT II: OSI and TCP/IP Models

    This unit explains the OSI (Open Systems Interconnection) and TCP/IP (Transmission Control Protocol/Internet Protocol) models, which are frameworks for understanding how data is transmitted over networks. It covers the layers of each model, their functions, and how they work together to enable communication between devices. The unit also highlights the similarities and differences between the two models.


    UNIT III: Routing and Switching

    This unit explores the core concepts of routing and switching in computer networks. It covers how switches connect devices within a local network and how routers direct data between different networks. Topics include routing protocols, types of routing (static and dynamic), switch operation, VLANs, and the role of routing and switching in efficient network communication.


    UNIT IV: Network Devices – Routers, Switches, and Hubs

    This unit focuses on essential networking hardware, including routers, switches, and hubs. It explains the functions and differences between these devices, how they manage data flow in networks, and their roles in both small and large-scale network infrastructures.


    UNIT V: Wireless Networking

    This unit introduces wireless networking concepts, including Wi-Fi standards, access points, and wireless security. It covers how wireless networks operate, their benefits and challenges, and how they connect devices without physical cables.


    UNIT VI: Network Security Basics

    This unit provides an overview of network security principles and practices. Topics include common threats (like malware and phishing), security protocols (such as firewalls and encryption), and basic measures to protect data and ensure safe communication over networks.


    Let's Try This!


    Activity Title: “Build Your Own Network!”

    Objective:
    Learners will apply basic concepts of computer networks—like topology, protocols, IP addressing, and hardware components—by designing a small office/home network.

    Instructions:
    Scenario:
    You are hired by a small startup company with 10 employees. They need a basic computer network setup to share files, access the internet, and connect printers. Your task is to design and describe a network that fulfills their needs.

    Activity Tasks:

    1. Identify Network Components (10 mins)
    • List the basic hardware you need: routers, switches, cables, computers, etc.
    • Explain their role in your setup.

    2. Choose a Network Topology (10 mins)
    • Select a topology (star, bus, ring, mesh, hybrid).
    • Justify your choice (e.g., cost, scalability, reliability).

    3. Assign IP Addresses (10 mins)
    • Use private IP addresses (e.g., 192.168.x.x).
    • Assign a unique IP for each device.

    4. Define Protocols Used (10 mins)
    • Mention 3–4 key protocols (e.g., TCP/IP, HTTP, DHCP, FTP).
    • Describe their functions in your network.

    5. Create a Network Diagram (optional - 10 mins)
    • Draw your network setup on paper or using a tool (e.g., draw.io, Lucidchart).
    • Show how devices connect.

    Deliverables:
    Each group/individual should present:
    • A short write-up or presentation covering all 5 tasks.
    • A diagram of the network (if applicable).

    Evaluation Criteria:

    Criteria Points
    Identification of components 10
    Topology choice & reasoning 10
    IP addressing accuracy 10
    Protocol understanding 10
    Presentation/diagram 10
    Total 50

    Extension / Homework (Optional):
    • Research and compare LAN, MAN, and WAN.
    • List 3 real-world examples of each type.
